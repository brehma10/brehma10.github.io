The Wakening, book 2

The latest news on the book will usually be found in the most recent blog article. There’s are exhaustive updates here and more recently here. If you want to know more about the writing process, you’ll find a detailed description here (also recently updated).

A short spoiler-free Q&A

Q: The question on everybody’s lips, Jonathan, is: When will book 2 be released? The correct answer is tomorrow, preferably before 7am. 8am is less ideal, but still acceptable.

A: [Laughter] Well, if I could work a few thousand hours tonight to make the 7am deadline, I certainly would. I’m cautious to give a release date until I’m close to finished. There are parts of the process that are more like digging a well than building a tower. Sometimes I need to dig far deeper than anticipated, but when the water begins gurgling in, I know it will be worth it for the reader. Initially, I did put out an estimate for a release, but I ran into this well-depth problem during the rewrites. The sequel has grown into a far bigger and more involved book than the first draft suggested. At this point, it’s over 1000 pages, and further-reaching than the debut in several senses. As a result, it will take longer to complete, but the result will be a much more satisfying read. I’ll provide a date as soon as everyone in the team agrees we’re close.

Q: Is that the cover for the next book?
A: No, just a little glimpse at one of the places in the sequel. This one was vaguely hinted at in the first book. I wonder if you can guess what I’m referring to.

Q: Let’s leave that for the readers. Can you tell us anything about the places, characters or mysteries?
A: Book 2, as you would know from book 1, is going to spend a lot of time on foreign waters and foreign soil. The places are quite different to those seen in the first book – colder, harsher, more hostile. We’ll be reunited with many of the important characters from the first book, now well-advanced in their skills and ready for the most intimidating challenge of their lives. The new characters were some of my favourites to write. I’m itching to introduce them. As to the mysteries … well, they need to be discovered, not explained.

Q: How will book 2 differ from book 1?
A: Where the first book had much to do with preparation, this is far more about putting skills to use. It’s time for the friends to learn if their training will keep them alive in a place that would very much prefer it if they were all dead. There’s still a good deal of the mischief that characterised the apprentices’ interactions, but where the first book was primarily a coming-of-age tale, this is much more of an adventure.

Q: What kind of Aedan can we expect?
A: Aedan is more mature and has a deeper sense of purpose. There are also some very interesting changes to him that have a strong bearing on the course of events. Of these, I am going to stubbornly refuse to say more.

Q: Does he find you-know-who?!
A: Wasn’t there something about no spoilers earlier?

Q: Okay, let’s move on to something entirely different then. You like to take your research into practical areas, such as testing out a bow-making chapter in the first book. Not so long ago you mentioned going to Israel for some additional Krav Maga training and writing inspiration. Do you have any more adventures/experiences planned to help with the writing of the second book?
A: I’ve been looking into crewing on a longship. I did a bit of sailing a few years ago and had some interesting experiences that involved oil tankers, sharks, and a skipper with a death wish. But all that sailing was done on more modern craft. I’d love to get a feel for what ocean life was like on traditional tall ships, especially the old square riggers, but right now I’m trying to put in as much writing as I can, so I haven’t booked anything. When I feel the need for a break, a sea voyage is going to be at the top of the list of options.

The Wakening, book 3

Q: How much of the story do you already know?
A: I have a rough plot-map in mind and few scenes imagined, but it’s only when I write the first draft that I’ll put on the bramble-scarred boots and start clearing a path with the machete. The first draft for me is about exploring and discovering, so I try not to think too much into the details until I’m ready to set out. I have written a few chapters, but only because the ideas were spilling out and I had to do something with them.

Q: You’ve spoken about the possibility of epic battles in this one. Are you planning on doing any hands-on research?
A: Oh yes. Oh absolutely yes! You get these medieval clubs in which combatants strap on traditional armour and engage in bouts with blunted weapons. The bouts range from polite tapping to enthusiastic pounding.

Q: I’m sure we’d all love to see this! Will you show us the video?
A: Depends on whether I manage to keep all my teeth or not.

Q: The one in which you lose your teeth would have great PR value.
A: Silence.

Q: Will the series end at the third book or will there be more?
A: This remains to be seen … A fourth looks likely, but it’s not certain yet.

    1. Jonathan Renshaw April 3, 2020 at 12:36 pm

      Thanks Eric. That’s inspiring to know 🙂 It’s a good question and I’m not sure if I can answer it satisfactorily, but I’ll give you my thoughts. For starters, I have changed career a few times. There were jobs that bled me dry and leaving them is something I don’t regret. Having said that, I don’t think any job is without dry patches. I’ve found I have to be intentional about inspiration. There’s of it in a full, balanced, and rested life. But sometimes it’s scarce no matter what you do. I remember Christopher Paulini commenting on this once, saying that if he waited for inspiration he would get almost nothing done. On the one side there’s the feeling of being bled dry, on the other that glorious spark of energising inspiration. I think normal is the space between where we allow for inspiration as best we can, but in its absence we content ourselves with taking pleasure in a hard day’s work well done. If that’s impossible, well, those were the times I changed career. Please don’t take this as career guidance! These are just my thoughts based on my own experiences. I hope the perspective is helpful.
